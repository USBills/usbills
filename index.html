April 13th, 2026 to April 19th, 2026

H.R. 6387 (FIRE ACT) is a bill that focuses on clarifying state air quality planning and stops states from being penalized by the EPA under the Clean Air Act when faced with air pollution from uncontrollable events. For example, under this bill, states prone to uncontrollable wildfires would not face EPA penalties. Furthermore, this bill mandates the EPA to create a public website describing the status of petitions regarding these exceptional events. This bill benefits local government, manufacturers, and agricultural interests by not punishing them for natural/uncontrollable air pollution.

H.R. 6398 (RED Tape Act) is a bill that focuses on amending the Clean Air Act by removing duplicate lettering and messaging in the Act itself. The main purpose is to remove the EPA's requirement of conducting environmental impact reviews on federal projects, federal agency actions, and proposed regulations that have already been environmentally approved by other agencies. This bill benefits construction/infrastructure developers who don't need to do the same environmental review twice and can now quickly cut through red tape in order to build.

H.R. 6409 (FENCES Act) is a bill that focuses on amending the Clean Air Act to protect states from EPA penalizations when they receive air pollution from foreign sources. If a state faces air emissions that they cannot control or emissions from outside American borders, they will not face sanctions, penalties, or federal funding delays for infrastructure. The Act also specifies that states need to update their emission demonstrations every five years. This bill benefits local and state governments, especially on the Southern border, from fines and obtaining "nonattainment" designations by the EPA. It also benefits energy and manufacturing producers from facing fines caused by emissions that they can not control.

H. Res. 1156 is a resolution that focuses on expressing support for the tax policies that benefit working families. It expresses support for supporting working families to keep more of their hard-earned money and commends the Working Families Tax Cuts introduced under the One Big Beautiful Bill. It benefits working families by delivering both tax credits and deductions that they can use to lower the amount owed.
